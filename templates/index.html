Mission

Student debt has become a significant burden for many students and recent graduates. Rising tuition costs, living expenses, and the increasing necessity of higher education have led to more students taking out loans to finance their studies. In many countries, especially in the United States, the average student graduates with tens of thousands of dollars in debt. This debt can impact their financial stability, career choices, and life decisions long after graduation. In turn, FundEd has brought it upon itself to combat student loans via educating students.

Debt Administration

There are two main types of loans: Fixed Plans and Income-Driven Repayment. fixed repayment plans, including Standard, Graduated, and Extended, provide predictable, structured payments over a set period, ensuring loans are repaid within a defined timeframe. In contrast, income-driven repayment plans adjust monthly payments based on the borrower's income and family size, offering flexibility and potentially lower payments for those with limited financial resources. Understanding the differences between these plans can help borrowers choose the best strategy for managing their student loan debt.
